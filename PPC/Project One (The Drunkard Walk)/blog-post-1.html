What is the Drunkard's Walk?

The random walk, often refered to as the drunkard's walk, is a mathematical concept that described a series of steps or movements in which each step is taken in a random direction, often with equal probability of moving in different directions. The concept is used to model various processes in fields such as finance, physics and statistics. In a simple random walk, the outcome of each step is independent of previous steps, making it a stochastic process. It's called a "walk" because it resembles the idea of a person taking steps in random directions, which can result in an unpredictable path or trajectory.
